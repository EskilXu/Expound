# Audience Persona: The Lawyer

## Who this is

Mid-career attorney, 8–18 years out of law school. Either in-house counsel at a mid-to-large enterprise, or on partner track at a firm. Time is the primary constraint — billable hours, or the equivalent pressure in-house. Has heard that AI will transform legal work, has tried ChatGPT or Claude once or twice, and formed a cautious opinion based on one experience that may or may not have been representative.

## What their work actually involves

- Reading and annotating long documents: contracts, regulations, briefs, due diligence materials
- Drafting: memos, correspondence, contract language, internal policy documents
- Comparative analysis: how does jurisdiction A's treatment of X differ from jurisdiction B's?
- Research: finding relevant precedent, regulatory guidance, agency interpretations
- Communicating: translating complex legal concepts for non-lawyer clients and colleagues

## Where Claude could add genuine value

- First-pass reading of long documents (flagging structure, identifying key provisions)
- Drafting first drafts of routine correspondence and internal memos
- Comparative analysis across documents (with verification)
- Research orientation: identifying the right questions and where to look, not the answers themselves

## The specific fears that create resistance

**Citation accuracy**: Lawyers know — viscerally — that a hallucinated case citation is a career risk. The fear is not abstract. One wrong citation in a brief or memo that gets sent to a client or filed in court is not a learning opportunity; it's a problem.

**Jurisdiction and date sensitivity**: Law is jurisdictionally specific and changes over time. Claude's training data has a cutoff; regulations are amended; court interpretations evolve. A lawyer who uses Claude for research and doesn't verify current law is practicing below the standard of care.

**Confidentiality**: Pasting client materials into a third-party AI system raises professional responsibility questions. This is a real barrier that the workflow collection does not fully resolve — it's a matter for each practitioner's firm or organization to decide.

## How to design for this persona

Every workflow used by this persona must:
1. Make citation verification explicit — not suggested, mandatory
2. Flag jurisdiction and effective date as live concerns, not one-time checks
3. Position Claude as a research accelerator and drafting partner, not a source of legal conclusions
4. Acknowledge the confidentiality concern honestly rather than dismissing it
